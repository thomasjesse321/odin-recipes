Ground Beef Bulgogi (quick and painless!)

Description

This ground beef bulgogi dish only takes 15 minutes to cook, is super budget friendly, and a perfect fallback for when you you just don't feel like it! This recipe only calls for a measly 11 ingredients total, but is so savory and delicious it'll fit right into your regular rotation!

Whether you're hankerin' for a hearty korean dish or just need to eat but don't feel like using your brain, you've found the perfect recipe. The Bold bulgogi flavors paired with the juicy beef make for a fantastic little trip for your taste buds! I highly recommend serving this dish over fluffy white rice or with a side of steamed veggies to make a complete meal!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef, preferably 90% lean
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 green onion,finely chopped, optional
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped

For Bulgogi Sauce

  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • 2 tbsp sweet rice wine (mirim)
  • 2-3 tbsp brown sugar

Steps

  1. Mix all bulgogi sauce ingredients in a small bowl and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a skillet over medium high heat. Add onion and garlic, and cook until soft, about 1-2 minutes. Add ground beef and cook until beef is browned and fully cooked.
  3. Pour the sauce mixture over the beef and stir to combine. Allow the mixture to simmer until it is heated through and the sauce reduces, about 2 minutes. Garnish with green onion and sesame seeds, if desired. Serve immediately.

Serving Suggestions

For a simple yet satisfying meal, sprinkle a modest amount of this meaty topping over a bowl of steaming rice or hearty multigrain rice.

Pair with your choice of fresh vegetables or a side of kimchi. You can also compliment this dish with an assortment of traditional Korean side dishes for a complete, flavor-packed meal.

Storage and reheating tip

Store leftover beef in the refrigerator for up to 1 week. You can also freeze it in a freezer-safe zip bag for up to 3 months. To reheat, either microwave to heat through or cook in a skillet with a little bit of oil over the stove top.
